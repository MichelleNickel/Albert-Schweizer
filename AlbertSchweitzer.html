Albert Schweitzer

Albert Schweizer, born on 14.01.1875, was a theologian, organist, musicologist, writer, humanitarian, philosopher, and physician.
He challenged the traditional christian views, won the Nobel Peace Price in 1952 for his philosophy of "Reverence for Life", influenced the Organ Reform Movement and founded and sustained the Albert Schweitzer Hospital in Lambaréné.

Oil on Canvas Portrait by Émile Schneider, 1912

    His Life

  • 1875

    • Birth on 14.01.1875 in Kaysersberg. Move to Günsbach in the same Year.

  • 1880-1893

    • Albert attends the Volksschule, Realschule, Gymnasium and takes lessons for the Organ from Eugen Munch.

  • 1893

    • On the 18th of June he gets his Matura degree and moves to his uncles in Paris, where he starts his studies in Theology and Philosophy and meets the Organist Charles Marie Widor

  • 1894-1896

    • Military service in the Infanterieregieme 143 in Strassbourg. In 1896 he decides to enter the "immediate human service" when he turns 30 years old.

  • 1898-1900

    • Finishing his studies in Theology and Philosophy, earning the PhDs in both subjects and continuing taking lessons in Piano and Organ.

  • 1902-1904

    • Lecturing Theology at the Strassbourg fakulties. He was even promoted to the director of the seminar. Meanwhile he writes his book "J. S. Bach, le musicien-poète".

  • 1905-1911

    • Release of his book dedicated to J. S. Bach, along with multiple books in the topics theology and philosophy. He is exceptionally active in Paris in the "Bachgesellschaft" and decides to study medicine.
    In october 1911 Schweitzer absolves his "Staatsexamen".

  • 1912

    • Because of exhaustion he moves back home to Günsbach and has to postpone his first trip to Afrika. On the 18.06.1912 he marries Helene Bresslau.

  • 1913

    • In February Albert earns his PhD in medicin and moves in may to Lambarene with wife, where they build their first Spital.

  • 1914-1918

    • Because of the war he was held back in europe for half a year. Also he fell into debt, trying to keep the Spital afloat.
    In 1915 he wrote his Notion "Respect for Life", wich became his life philosophy. In 1918 they returned to Elsass, where Albert was operated on for the first time and started working as an assistent in the klinik and he resumed his position as Vikar in the St. Nicolao pnce again.

  • 1917

    • They are considered prisoners of war, but are send back to france, to live in a Casern in Bordeaux and then send on to Hautes Pyrénées.

  • 1919-1921

    • Birth of his daughter, another operation on Albert and a successfull touné for his musik and his philosophies. Switzerlands Erzbishof soederblom invites him to be a Professor in the University of Zurich, but Schweitzer declines to persue his work in Lambarene.
    With the money he collected during his tour he can pay off his debts and return to Lambarene. He started being a village pastor and continues his concerts and his culture philosophie.

  • 1923-1926

    • His wife has fallen ill with tuberculosis because of the internment and cannot join him to Lambarene. He builds a house for her and his daughter in the Black Forest and goes with Noël Gillespie, a geology and chemistry student from oxford.
    During the next few years more and more poeple join his Spital, nurses and doctors! Because of the little space he dicides to build another Spital three kilometers up the stream.

  • 1927-1928

    • The Spital is moved to where it resides today. The same year he is holding speeches in sweden, denmark and germany. The year after he continues with germany, netherlands, switzerland and great britain.
    August 28th 1927 the city of Frankfurt awards him the Goethe Prize. With the prizemoney he builds a house in his hometown for his office.
    in Prague on december 5th he recieves the title of doctor honoris causa from the Faculty of Philosophy. He gives a few more concerts and speeches and eats lunch with the president of the Czechoslovak Republic, Thomas Garrigue Masaryk.

  • 1929-1939

    • Schweitzer travels to Lambarene twice more, once in 1929 and once in 1933. His wife has to return after jsut three months because of her worsening wellbeing.
    Schweitzer tours through europe again in 1932, 1934 and 1936. Through the next years he publishes a few books, e.g. "The great thinkers of India", tours throughj europe or Travels back to afrika, where he stayed long periods of time, even years at some point.
    Helene and Rhéna travel through the United States to advertise for her husbands work.

  • 1939-1946

    • After two weeks in Europe, to buy medicine and provisions he returns to Lambarene and stays there for almost 10 years. He thinks the upcoming war is unfortunatley inevitable, but even when the Vichy-Groups and the "free frenchmen" start fighting each other close to his Spital, he remains neutral to both parties and tends to both soldiers the same. 1941 his wife finally makes it to Lambarene via Portugal and Angola, but she returns to Europe in 1946.
    Sweden and the USA support his Spital during these years with medication and food.
    1948 Schweitzer returns to Bordeaux.

  • 1949-1950

    • The Schweitzers travel to the USA, where Albert holds a speech to honor Goethe on his 200th birthday in Aspen/Colorado. With the money he earned there, they make their way to Lambarene, where they start building a Lepra-village.

  • 1951

    • Return to Europe. In September he earns the "Peaceprize of the german bookmarket" in Frankfurt and he is elected into the "Académie des Sciences morales et politique" after the passing of Marshall Pétiain.
    At the end of the year he is once again traveling to Lambarene for seven months.

  • 1952-1953

    • On the 30th october 1953 he is awared the Nobelprize for peace for the year 1952. With the money he is able to finish the construction "du Village lumière".

  • 1954-1955

    • Albert Schweitzer is accepted into the Academy of Art and Science as a special member in the USA. He earns many Prizes and "special mentions" from all over the world on his 80th Birthday.
    At the end of those two years he travels back to Lambarene, his wife accompanies him on the second trip.

  • 1957-1958

    • On the 23. April ALbert talks about the dangers of the Atomic Bombs on the Radio in Oslo. Those three speeches are later published as a book.
    In may his wife, Helene, travels back to Europe via a plane and sadly passes away on the first of June 1957 at the age of 79. Albert stays in Europe for four months, before returning to Lambarene.

  • 1959

    • Schweizer travels for three months through Europe and stays a month in Paris, before leaving for Lambarene for the 14th and last time.

  • 1965

    • On the 14th January he celebrates his 90th birthday, but around August his strength is quickly leaving him, leading to his passing on the 4th of september at 23:30.
    The next day he is burried next to his wife in Lambarene.

Albert Schweitzer was an extraordinary person and his lifestory speaks for itself. He was incredibly talented and intelligent, but always stayed humble and cared for human life and the people around him. Even when life tried to push him down, he was in debt, captured and had to endure humiliation and bad living circumstances, he prevailed and kept on looking into a better future.
Let's all try to let his legacy and philosophies live on in all of us.
